# IJDS two-ruler paper-facing interpretation erratum

## Scope

This note corrects paper-facing interpretation after the locked V1c/V2 runs.
It does not alter either protocol, config, tag, allocation, outcome, metric, or
artifact. The immutable records remain the source of provenance; this note is
the source of wording for any integrated manuscript.

## Finite grid, not complete continuous support

V1c evaluated the complete declared interior grid
`coordinate={.25,.50,.75}` under two outcome-free rulers. It did not enumerate
the continuous two-ruler frontier. Earlier promotion language requiring one
nonzero sign over the "complete common frontier support" was therefore too
broad. The implemented gate covers the complete **declared three-coordinate
grid** only.

Coordinate one is also a structural null for the endpoint contrast. The
normalized-score cap reaches each endpoint score's common unconstrained
plug-in optimum, and the objective-matched floor reaches the same verified
unique plug-in optimum. Both `gamma=0` and `gamma=1` therefore return the common
objective-optimal allocation up to solver tolerance. No universal nonzero sign
can hold over the literal closed coordinate interval `[0,1]`.

The exact V4 point-cap basis frontier remains the support-wide triangulation.
V2 is a finite-grid sensitivity that falsifies ruler/coordinate invariance at
the declared interior points; it is not an exact continuous-frontier result.

## Six tracks, not replications

The 48 complete-window cells are six ruler-coordinate tracks conditioned on
eight overlapping residual recipes and evaluated on the same fifteen OOT
monthly menus and outcomes. They are not 48 replications, and direction counts
are a specification census rather than a vote.

Objective-matched `.25` is effectively one repeated allocation contrast. Each
window has the same four active months and eleven structural zeros. Across USD
15 million of monthly budgets, the contrast changes 44 loan-month positions,
has about USD 155,937 one-way turnover, USD 5,603.66 higher standardized payoff,
and 0.00679 percentage points lower funded default and miscoverage. The eight
window aggregates differ only by solver noise. Paper-facing prose must report
this once, not as eight confirmations.

## Outcome-join incident and hardening

The artifact-producing V2 invocation verified the freeze, performed one join,
and wrote the complete immutable result. A client timeout left that child
process alive; a second invocation repeated the fixed evaluation in memory and
then stopped at the no-overwrite check. It wrote no artifact and did not change
the specification or select a result.

Consequently, paper-facing wording is "one artifact-producing evaluation," not
an unqualified claim that the evaluator was invoked exactly once. A post-run
hardening change adds an output-path preflight before archive loading for any
future run tag. The V2 tag continues to preserve the code that actually
produced the retained artifacts.

## Permitted conclusion

Within a predeclared finite diagnostic, the `gamma=1 - gamma=0` allocation
contrast is not invariant to two outcome-free rulers or three interior
coordinates. Objective matching yields one small four-month favorable contrast
at `.25`, an unfavorable contrast at `.50`, and mostly unidentified
payoff/default contrasts at `.75`. The normalized ruler is uniformly
unfavorable in realized outcomes but does not match plug-in opportunity cost.

This identifies no preferred gamma, ruler, coordinate, or policy. It does not
provide a causal effect, sampling confidence interval, complete-frontier
ordering, funded-set conformal guarantee, or repair of temporal transport.
